The Mobile Impact team is skilled at generating in-depth descriptions of new products and services. We have successfully translated product plans into detailed product descriptions and implementable product requirements. Throughout the project, we collaborate with the client team to extract the fundamental features to create descriptions and use cases that drive requirements, while maintaining traceability throughout the process. These steps form the link between strategic product/service plans and successful implementation of the solution. We develop requirements at all levels, as needed by our clients, from market requirements through product requirements and into engineering functional requirements.

  • Products and Services Description: Together with our clients, we develop in depth, accurate descriptions of products and services that highlight features and functionality in a format easy to communicate and socialize throughout the organization. These descriptions form the basis for product and service positioning, marketing plans, and executive approval of the project.
  • Use Cases: Fundamental to developing requirements, use cases drive the expected performance of any product or service. We work with the client to create a complete description of the product or service by documenting use cases. We then map the use cases into tangible and implementable requirements.
  • Requirements Generation: Requirements provide a blueprint for the end solution, while not specifically prescribing a given implementation. From them, Mobile Impact can determine detailed project schedules, resource projections, and costs. The requirements, also, provide the guidelines for our team to manage the development of the solution, while maintaining clear traceability to the original use cases and requirements.
  • Engineering Design Document (Functional Requirements Generation): Implementation starts with a strong set of requirements and functional requirements form the basis of “how” a solution will be designed. Whether the product or service is implemented by the client or outsourced, the Mobile Impact team can provide the functional requirements, system architecture, or technical specifications for the product/service, which are then translated into a specific technical design that identifies system impacts, equipment utilization, and detailed functionality.
  • Requirement Management and Traceability: Generating connected and coherent documentation for any new product or service is essential to achieving expectations of the entire organization for any product or service and managing the inevitable trade-offs that arise in the product life cycle. The Mobile Impact team uses techniques that connect the product description, use cases, requirements, specifications, and engineering design. This traceability is then used to accurately identify the source requirements and communicate to team members about any adaptations required to support the new product or service. It also serves as the foundation for future product/service evolution by capturing requirements planned for future iterations and upgrades.
