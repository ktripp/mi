The Mobile Impact team is truly unique in our ability to rapidly deliver a wide range of projects in marketing, engineering, procurement, or operations. We believe that the collective knowledge and combined experience of our team delivers greater value than traditional contracting approaches.

Our team is ready to provide high impact, real-world results with a dedication to quality and a focus on delivery. Our mission is to provide high quality, strategic, technical, and business consulting solutions by means of sharing and applying our knowledge and experience from the wireless ecosystem.

Shawn D. Bayle

Partner and President

Shawn has over 20 years of experience in the mobile device market with a diverse background in handset business management and application deployment. Shawn has led commercial development, sales, product management, and marketing of many new product lines across the wireless ecosystem. Shawn founded Mobile Impact in early 2007 to serve wireless clients in recognition of the rapidly growing but fluid mobile data market.

Previously, Shawn was VP of Business Development at TeleworX, Inc. where he built a mobile data practice and grew the existing transport engineering services business. Previously, Shawn worked at Ericsson, Inc. in a variety of management roles including: Core Technology, Handset Product Management, Handset and Accessory Sales Management, and Network Applications Systems Sales. Shawn's responsibilities grew from Product Manager up to Vice President. During his tenure, he managed the overhaul of the AMPS handset product line, launched a line of CDMA handsets, and landed multiple contracts valued at over $100 Million. His product responsibility included handsets, accessories, Intellectual Property licensing, and mobile data infrastructure and systems. Shawn managed sales teams and major account relationships for Tier 1 mobile operators, national retailers, and chipset vendors.

Additionally, Shawn worked in mobile chipset marketing for AT&T Microelectronics and embedded processor applications engineering for NEC Electronics. Shawn led the commercial development and launch of numerous chipsets in the cellular, voice processing, and embedded control markets.

Shawn holds a MS Computer Engineering degree from Boston University, a BS EE with a concentration in Computer Science from Lafayette College, and numerous MBA courses in Leadership, Business Management, and Product Marketing. Shawn authored US and international patents.

Jeff Tripp

Partner and Principal

email: jtripp@mobileimpactgroup.com


Jeff has over 20 years experience in the wireless industry, possessing a broad background in product/project management, systems engineering, IC development, product integration and realization. He has assembled, managed and led multi-disciplinary teams focused on mobile wireless product realization and product introduction.

Jeff has a BS in Computer Engineering from University of Illinois and an MBA from the DePaul University. He has provided hands-on consulting to wireless operators and been involved on a variety of technical and business consulting projects that includes product/service requirements and use case generation, supply chain management (vendor management, device procurement, sales and operations planning tools, RFI/RFP), handset sourcing and product cost analysis/break-downs, near field communications (NFC, RFID), and portable data applications (Java, Web-browser, user interface), application integration, and content distribution.

Jeff has served as the Vice President of Mobile Strategy and Technology at Teleworx, Inc providing consulting services, and previously, worked at Tyco as Director of Business Development, where he established an IC design center focused on new wireless devices and architectures. At Tyco, he assembled engineering and business teams, directed product requirements, market analysis, and customer contact, as well as assisted engineering with project management and development. Prior to that Jeff was both a Director of Product Management and Director of Engineering at Motorola. Jeff has developed mobile devices from concept through market deployment, from development of technical/system requirements through implementation of both integrated circuits and integration of mobile devices. He has managed new product introduction teams, handset/IC development teams, and has a deep understanding of mobile wireless products, features and marketplace.

He holds 4 patents and has filed for several others.

Joy Maguire

Principal

Joy brings over 20 years of expertise in wireless & wireline telecommunications services: cellular handset and systems, WLAN, personal area, optical and intelligent networks, wireless Internet devices, applications, technologies and standards. As high-tech industry veteran, Joy has held executive roles in Product and Business Management, has maintained a deep presence in Industry Standards groups, and has consulted with industry leaders since 2003.

Previously, she was Vice President of Marketing at C Speed Corporation, a developer of MEMS-based optical switching subsystems. Prior to C Speed, she served as Vice President of Global Wireless and Mobility Solutions for Razorfish and has previously been Sr. Director of Product Management, Marketing and Business Development for Mitsubishi Wireless Communications, Inc. where she developed and launched the world's first commercial Smartphones (1st & 2nd generation Internet-ready MobileAccess™ phones) and developed partnerships with carriers, infrastructure vendors, software developers, system integrators and value added resellers. Before joining Mitsubishi Electronics, Joy held product management, market management and business development positions at Centigram Communications Corporation and AT&T. At Centigram, she developed and delivered an Enhanced Services consulting program for carriers and network operators.

Joy is a current member of the WCA RFID SIG, the WIC Enterprise, and M2M Committees and chaired the CTIA Enterprise Action Team sub-committee on Standards and Industry Groups in 2002/3. In 1999-01, Joy founded and chaired the WAP Forum Specification Requirements Committee (SRC), which developed and prioritized market requirements for next generation specifications. She served from 1998-2001 on the Wireless Data Forum's Board and chaired the ISDN User Forum Messaging and Answering Team from 1993-96. In March 2000, Wireless Week recognized Ms. Maguire as one of the most influential women in wireless.

Richard "Skip" Bryan

Principal

Skip is a seasoned consultant and technologist with over 25 years experience in terrestrial and satellite wireless communications, business management, and engineering covering GSM-TDMA, microwave communications, 802.xx, Bluetooth, wireless services, and mobile Internet solutions. He has developed device and service specifications including functional requirements for device testing, device middleware and application environments, mobile browsing, and has developed, executed, and evaluated RFPs and vendor bake-offs for mobile device applications and operator data services.

Prior to consulting, Skip spent 9 years at Ericsson in a variety of management roles in both the mobile phone and systems divisions, starting as an engineering manager for Ericsson's handset team, designing the world's smallest dual mode digital handset. Subsequently he became Director of Product Management for data accessories where he served as a founder of and Director on the WAP Forum Board. He authored the wireless data strategic plan for client-server data wireless systems and was responsible for mobile ecosystem roadmaps to address the emerging smart phone and applications market. Later, he served both as Director of Data Technology Marketing and Americas' Area Manager for Technology Licensing. Moving to the Ericsson Systems division, he provided strategic planning for data applications services guiding strategy development leading to: the deployment of ADA-compliant mobile phones, Smart Phone functional requirements, virtual wireless network operations models, and operator wireless applications including ring tones, music sales, and mobile positioning services.

Prior to Ericsson, Mr. Bryan was Engineering Vice President at ST Systron Donner Corporation holding responsibility for microwave communications components, subsystems, and instrumentation for military communications and electronic warfare products. Previously he worked at Hughes Aircraft Company where he held a number of engineering roles responsible for wireless and communication systems including design of microwave communication satellite receivers, frequency synthesizer subsystems, and transmitter circuits. His responsibility included design, production development, and test and verification. Skip holds both BS and MS degrees in Electrical Engineering and has served on several industry standards groups including: the IEEE Microwave Theory and Techniques Society (President in 1997), the Bluetooth Special Interest Group, and the WAP Forum.

Mariko Sakai

Director

Mariko is a telecom professional with over 18 years of experience focused in the areas of carrier operations, product development, supply chain management, and project management. Mariko is particularly skilled at driving solutions across a wide array of business areas including operations, pricing, finance, IT, supply chain management, engineering, and product management.

Mariko has provided consulting services for the past 7 years. Most recently, she has worked on projects to improve carrier device certification performance, to define requirements to deploy a Sales & Operations Planning tool and processes, to develop large scale handset purchase contracts, to document and improve service and repair processes, and to break down and analyze product costs. She has drafted and negotiated multiple contracts to purchase wireless products and services, helping clients achieve significant cost savings. Previously, Mariko was a Product Manager at Cable and Wireless USA where she was responsible for all aspects of product development and management, from generating product requirements, order to bill processes, to marketing collateral, for voice and data products. Before this, she worked at Iridium both as a Pricing Manager and Product Manager, defining and implemented pricing, rating, and billing requirements as well as voice service enhancements.

Mariko has a global experience base working with clients and customers in over 20 countries. She is fluent in English and Japanese, and is proficient in Spanish. She holds a Masters in International Economics, with concentration in Energy, Environment, Science and Technology from the Johns Hopkins University School of Advanced International Studies, and a BA in Business from Keio University in Tokyo, Japan.
